Sensors

The FarmBot platform integrates a growing suite of advanced sensors to monitor plant health, soil conditions, and overall greenhouse performance. Below are the primary sensor systems used in the BotanyBot project:

  • NDVI Camera: Tracks plant vigor, stress, and chlorophyll density using near‑infrared analysis.
  • Luxonis OAK‑D‑Pro: Depth + AI vision for plant detection, canopy measurement, weed identification, and tool alignment.
  • UWB Positioning Nodes: Precise real‑time location tracking for autonomous movement validation.
  • Soil Moisture Sensors: Measures root‑zone hydration to automate accurate watering.
  • Soil Nutrient Sensors: Nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium (NPK) monitoring for smarter fertilization cycles.
  • Ambient Environment Sensors: Temperature, humidity, CO₂, and light intensity tracking for greenhouse climate optimization.

More sensors will be added as the research expands, including spectral imaging modules and upward/downward reflective NDVI baselines.
